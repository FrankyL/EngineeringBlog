Concert LED Glow Stick

Franky Laurentis August 30, 2025

I forgot where I misplaced my old glow stick so I thought: Eh why not let's try to make one

Pic above is all the printed parts, I managed to get my hands on this extra dense (144 LEDs per meter) LED cable strip that already have silicone (I think it's silicone?)

First need to connect the LED stick into the center core

It felt a bit wobbly to me so I hotglue'd the stick to the center core

The Handle here have a hole that fits exactly the same place so that the wire from LED strip can go through. For now, it's not screwed in yet

Connect the ESP32 Supermini to the LED wires, had to bend the header pins cuz I underestimated how long it is and this hurts me in my soul LOL

Use the battery pack to cover the ESP32 on the base handle, then screw in 2 screws onto the base handle (out of 4 screw holes, 2 works well for me)

Also, while I know I made it for 4x AAA but I end up actually just using 3, total of 4.5V should be OK for the LED strip and the regulator on ESP32 supermini but 6V might be risky

ME6211 (regulator on board the typical ESP32 supermini) should be able to work up to 6V but really can't guarantee it cuz this one is generic module off Aliexpress and the WS2812 too only said DC5V, so I ended up just playing it safe for now

Screwed the center core to the light cover and the handle

Picked up a new trick recently to just make a flat rectangular peg like this, slot in the battery cover and just screw the other end.

It works!

I should remove the red LED that comes by default with ESP32s, I think it drains the battery more than the ESP32 when it's in deep sleep mode. Also the reason why it defaults to red after the brightness goes to 0 is cuz it's in sleep mode, and pressing the button again wakes it up and it have to start from the 1st color, which is red.

It looks like one of those Jedi Light daggers LOL and the white looks so good.
