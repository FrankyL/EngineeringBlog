DIY Servo-Controlled Business Sign (Open, Closed, Opening Soon) + 3D Printed Gear Mechanism

Franky Laurentis July 11, 2025 3D printed gear mechanism

First time trying out 3D printing some gear mechanism then actuating it with servo. Janky as all hell but it worked out kind of. Other than the gear part, everything else is pretty straight forward, ESP32 + SG90 Servo + WS2812 LED strips, Wi-Fi to read NTP time, no comms or anything complicated.

The idea is to have something that can change the sign that a shop is closed, open or opening soon, then changes color according to which sign is being displayed.

"Wooden Gear Mechanism Convertible Open Closed Signs for Business" is the name for this item. Basically the same idea but I thought why not use servo? Then add a 3rd state called "opening soon"

 

Above is how it internally looks without the 3D plate, basically a central pillar for WS2812 LED strips to stick to, also triangular prism and I split it such that I am able to take out the central part without needing to disassemble the outer shell too

I swear it looks waaaayyyyy better IRL. This is the Open state, the plate is saying "OPEN 营业中". Maybe I should put a diffuser sheet behind the 3D-printed plate

This one looks clearer on camera maybe cuz I printed it first at a higher infill of 60% IIRC. The plate is saying "OPENING SOON 即将营业".

Man, this one looks the worst on camera even though it ain't that bad IRL. Supposed to say "CLOSED 已打烊“. Although I know prior to this that "closed" is something like "da yang" colloquially but this is the first time I see it in writing which is pretty neat. I got these labelling suggestions off of ChatGPT too haha, I chose the more casual/less formal ones.

Encountered so much trouble with the gearing mechanisms, I put the usual 0.3mm tolerance but end up not that good (Possible too my 3D printer having some issues with the X-Y axis, the tolerances are all out of whack). The 0.5cm pegs on the rotating prism went into the bearing on each end is also really weak even at 90% infill, I end up using soldering tip at 250C to melt the peg's layers together, then reinforcing some more with a small piece of filament.

I think I printed 3-4 times for the gears to trial and error which one works the best. If you are wondering why I went out of my way to use gears: The servo itself can only rotate from 0 to 180 deg, the prism in this case is 360/3 = 120 deg from one side to the next. Thus, at least i need to have something that can rotate from 0 to 240 deg cuz the prism need to be able to rotate through 3 states. I just used 2 to 1 gear ratio to expand the total rotation from 180 to 360.

YT vid above is the signage rotation test (i.e. literally just change between states). There's also a custom function to gradually change the servo angle, the original one is way too fast (basically just servo.write(targetAngle) with no intermediate angles) and it always end up oscillating at the correct position. You can notice too that the servo motion is smoother when I apply a bit of friction using my finger.

Either way I think I should just get a better servo haha, this SG90 is cheap AND old (2-3 years at least).

Currently the configuration array is something like this (I spent quite a bit of time trying to figure out the best angle for each state and even now Closed state still have a slightly awkward angle, it's worse at first cuz the best angle is smack dab right in the dead zone between 0 and 180, around 182 or -2(actually invalid inputs))
