N4 JLPT Revision Notes

Franky Laurentis June 5, 2025

I'm going to use this post as a way to list down the vocabs in JLPT N4 that might be hard to remember (for me). Also going to use ChatGPT to generate sentences that I can use to understand how to use them.

Sometimes I know them by pronunciation but can't remember it's the same thing when I encounter the kanji. Sometimes I know how to read but forgot the meaning.

The part where Japanese adopted Chinese characters as a way to pronounce their stuff absolutely complicates basically everything

Honestly speaking, learning a bit of Japanese is also kinda like a revision on Classical Chinese 文言文 (the older meaning and vocabs still used in dialects like Hokkien / Cantonese)

赤ん坊 (akanbou): baby, infant

案内 (annai): guidance, leading (the way)

浅い (asai): shallow, superficial

謝る (ayamaru): to apologize, the kanji also same for chinese thank you, xiexie, also use in 感謝 (kansha)

貿易 (boueki): trade (foreign)​

文学 (bungaku): literature, language-study

文法 (bunpou): grammar, language-methods --> grammar

注射 (chuusha): injection

駐車場 (chuushajou): parking lot

暖房 (danbou): (indoor) heating​, lit. warm-room

電報 (denpou): telegram, electric-report

電灯 (dentou): electric light​, need to remember the chinese "deng" becomes "tou"

泥棒 (dorobou): thief, burglar, robber, theft​, doro-dirt/filth, bou-stick/suffix in older japanese to denote a person. lit. the filthy one

遠慮 (enryo): constraint, restraint, modesty

増える (fueru): to increase, to multiply

踏む (fumu): to step on, to tread on​, "funderu" is more usually used

下宿 (geshuku): boarding, lodging

ご馳走 (gochisou): feast, treating (someone)​, uncommon kanji usage, go-chi-zhou, should be quite easy to remember

塵 (gomi): rubbish, trash, garbage, who tf uses the kanji for this? top part is deer and bottom is dirt?

拝見 (haiken): seeing, look at, bai-jian, chinese hint a bit at the onyomi

運ぶ (hakobu): to carry, to transport

冷える (hieru): to grow cold, need to remember the kunyomi, not "samueru"

引っ越す (hikkosu): to move (house)

拾う (hirou): to pick up

苛める (ijimeru): to bully, to torment, kanji uncommon, typically all kana

辞典 (jiten): dictionary, jisho is more commonly used, jiten is the formal one, indicating a more "encyclopedic formal documentation" kind of idea

海岸 (kaigan): coast, beach​, hai-an in chinese

掛ける (kakeru): to hang, to hoist, to raise

構う (kamau): to mind, to care about

家内 (kanai): (my) wife, jia-nei, quite similar in pronounciation, Casual speakers might find it old-fashioned; modern usage is mostly 家族 (kazoku) or 妻 (tsuma). indonesian have something similar, and chinese equivalent is 内人

片付ける (katazukeru): to tidy up, to put in order, tzukeru is pretty obvious, kata in front need to remember

乾く (kawaku): to get dry, Slang: In some contexts, can mean feeling thirsty (colloquial)

通う (kayou): to go back and forth between, 100% will often forget the kunyomi and pull the "tsuu-u" sound, Casual nuance: can imply more than physical movement (to regularly attend)

飾る (kazaru): to decorate, to ornament

経済 (keizai): economics, economy, jing ji in chinese

見物 (kenbutsu): sightseeing, watching, interesting vocab "view-things", thus sightseeing, Colloquial: can mean “spectator” or “watching an event,” sometimes even “watching a fight” (like “spectacle”).

厳しい (kibishii): severe, strict, rigid

決る (kimaru): to be decided, to be settled​

規則 (kisoku): rule, regulation​, gui ze in chinese

細かい (komakai): small, fine, minute

この頃 (kono goro): recently, nowadays

故障 (koshou): breakdown, out of order, gu zhang, zhang becomes shou

講堂 (koudou): auditorium, lecture hall​

暮れる (kureru): to get dark, to grow dark​

参る (mairu): to go, to come (humble), お宅へ参ります, also used in those swordfight animu scenes, they say "mairimasu!" to declare they are going to attack, この度は参りました(this one means "defeated", a more figurative use)

湖 (mizuumi): lake, ain't nobody remember how this is pronounced alone, everyone just use (name of lake)"ko"

申し上げる (moushiageru): to say, to tell (humble)

迎える (mukaeru): to go out to meet

向かう (mukau): to face, to go towards

昔 (mukashi): olden days, former

直る (naoru): to get mended vs 直す (naosu): to fix, to repair

値段 (nedan): price, cost

塗る (nuru): to paint, to plaster

盗む (nusumu): to steal​

お祝い (oiwai): congratulations, celebration, i know this already, just fun tidbit of "IWAE!!!"

折れる (oreru): to break, to be broken, to give in/yield to negotiations(figurative) vs 折る (oru): to break, to fracture

お釣り (otsuri): change (for a purchase)

留守 (rusu): absence, being away from home​, "remain-protection" weird way to say absence but okay. Used in expressions like 留守番 (rusu-ban) — “house-sitting” or “watching the house.”

坂 (saka): slope, hill, osaka means "big slope"

盛ん (sakan): popular, prosperous, implies popularity is long-term, not just fads, e.g. この町では農業が盛んです

昨夜 (sakuya): last night

政治 (seiji): politics, government​

叱る (shikaru): to scold, a more formal form of "scold" compared to okorareta, implies you kind of deserved it

支度 (shitaku): preparation, arrangements​, 出かける前に支度する, shitaku-suru or also works on its own as a noun

招待 (shoutai): invitation, zhao dai, zhao becomes shou, casual invites often use 誘う (sasou).

滑る (suberu): to glide, to slide, fail(like a joke falling flat), failing exam(slang), この道は雨の日に滑りやすい,彼のギャグは完全に滑った,試験に滑った

隅 (sumi): corner, nook, recess​

大抵 (taitei): mostly, ordinarily, 私は大抵7時に起きます(taitei 7ji, usually at 7), 大抵の人はそれを知らない(taitei no hito = most people)

棚 (tana): shelf, shelves

足りる (tariru): to be sufficient, to be enough​ vs 足す (tasu): to add, to top up

訪ねる (tazuneru): to visit, to call on vs 尋ねる (tazuneru): to ask, to enquire, kunyomi issue, need to remember the 1st tazuneru is not "houneru" or something similar

展覧会 (tenrankai): exhibition

泊まる (tomaru): to stay at (e.g. hotel)​

都合 (tsugou): circumstances, convenience​, chinese is very weird, du-he, meaning not obvious, remember STARTRIP's lyric 都合良く何度も夢に見る, tsuugou yoku nandomo yume ni miru, "tsugou yoku" means conveniently

釣る (tsuru): to fish, to lure in, remember the word for "change(money)" is o(tsuri), fishing for change, thus otsuri

包む (tsutsumu): to wrap up, to tuck in, implies gentle or complete wrapping, more than just putting something in a bag, おにぎりを海苔で包む, 静けさが町を包んでいた(figurative)

受付 (uketsuke): reception (desk), information desk​

旨い (umai): delicious, appetizing, ain't no one is using this kanji, btw 上手い、美味い both are pronounced the same way, 2nd one actually same meaning. “旨い” can be used more colloquially in masculine speech, slang for “good” or “cool” sometimes.

写す (utsusu): to transcribe, to duplicate, to take photo of (formal/poetic/flowery)ノートに先生の板書を写す, 風景を写す(formal form of "take photo"). In casual speech, 写真を撮る (shashin o toru) is used for “to take a photo.”

沸く (waku): to grow hot, to boil, Can mean “to become excited” or “to get lively” in some contexts.

割れる (wareru): to break, to be smashed vs 割合 (wariai): rate, ratio

汚れる (yogoreru): to get dirty, remember, pronounciation nothing to do with "kitanai", why? who knows.

寄る (yoru): to approach, to drop by, 船が岸に寄った, 帰りにコンビニに寄る, 彼は壁に寄りかかっている(leaning on something, more figurative), implies a short, casual stop — not a long visit

予習 (yoshuu): preparation for a lesson​

輸出 (yushutsu): export
