[NUS ME M.Sc Module Reviews] ME5406 Deep Learning for Robotics

Franky Laurentis June 5, 2025

I still have no idea how I get A+ for this mod

Everything was a messy blur and I worked until the final 30 minutes before submission deadline.

Deployed my PC, my bro's gaming laptop, and my laptop to crunch the drone simulation and reinforcement learning for basically 24/7 until the submission deadline.

Very Maths heavy and I was lost almost immediately, recovered with Youtube and ChatGPT. I would say this is less boring (at the least the group work) than ME5404Â Neural Networks.

Part 1 is dry, topics on Markov decision processes, dynamic programming, Monte-Carlo, SARSA, Q-learning and Deep-Q-Networks

Part 2 is more practical, Robot perception, Deep Q-learning and variants, Policy Gradient, Imitation learning, Motion/Path Planning, Articulated Robotics, and Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning.

Part 1 assessment is solo project on solving 4x4 grid maze, then 10x10 grid using 1st visit Monte-Carlo, SARSA and then Q-learning. Actually quite tough even with ChatGPT as it requires tuning the model repeatedly and 1st visit Monte Carlo is indeed supposed to be weaker than SARSA or Q-learning at this.

Part 2 assessment is "group work", and I worked with 2 china students. Have to say, they are pretty good at what they do, except 1. the module is assessed individually anyway with individual reports, and 2. they barely responds on Whatsapp so I can't really get the environment working (the idea is proposed by one of them, a PhD student) so supposedly, I think we're supposed to use his environment to train.

End up making a whole ass simulation with obstacles on PyBullet, the idea he proposed is to use Soft-Actor-Critic to softly manipulate Model Predictive Path Integral (MPPI) control of a drone. So if you know those 2 combinations of strange words, you will be like "but MPPI is already pretty good for obstacle avoidance, no?" in which I say, you're right.

So how are we supposed to tell SAC is suitable for controlling MPPI?

I dunno man, not really my idea, I just try to make this work. I would have liked to do a walking chicken robot reinforcement learning though, but alas, too late for that.

And yeah, my conclusion after the whole thing is like: "Looks pretty similar to me in performance, but here you go, this is the SAC code and training model, help yourself."

Managed to get several pretty solid Python scripts for training and testing the simulations after like a couple of weeks. I remember running the sim overnight and also remote in to my PC still running in SG as I went on vacation to Lake Toba in North Sumatra. Really chill place, I want to retire already, feelsbadman.

Main point is, by the gods just solo project 2, at least you will do something you give a shit about, instead of trying to make someone's PhD idea work.
