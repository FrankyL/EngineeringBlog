Franky Laurentis June 5, 2025

FINAL SEM WOOHOO

ME5506 Corrosion of Materials [2425-SEM2]

Interesting module to me. The module highlights various forms of corrosion and the countermeasures.

Really hits you with the "Yeah, corrosion happens everywhere, only difference is how fast, how severe, and how much foresight people put in to counter them"

Happens much faster with electricity flowing around. Guess what I usually do for a living? Electronics. No wonder, solders are a huge pain point for us.

Pic above from a different website, but shows the main point, corrosion happens regardless, just how slow and how often the countermeasures need to be reapplied

Assessed on 2 quizzes iirc, both open book, can bring laptop, just not phones. I think they just want to prevent people from communicating outside their group. Yes, 'group'. You are basically put into a group and you can work together to solve this open book assessment. Only catch is 1 hr 40 mins to solve like 10 open ended questions, good thing we are allowed to use ChatGPT or DeepSeek, but good luck writing down the paragraphs of answer if you work solo.

I worked with another guy, he took even questions, I took odd questions, then we ChatGPT/DeepSeek our questions, assess which one makes more sense according to what the prof has been teaching and quickly pass it back and forth writing down our answers. He write, I search-> I write, he search.

got a B+ whew

If you want to use the knowledge in your work, bro just ChatGPT it. There's like 8 different forms of corrosion and multiple variants under each for different environment and situations. Just ask ChatGPT to analyze the "hypothetical" situation and recommend what are you supposed to do to minimize the rate of corrosion, it usually gets it right.

ME5611 Sustainable Product Design & Manufacturing [2425-SEM2]

This one, I have to admit sounds like the most "meme" of the modules I took in M.Sc. But surprisingly dense on the materials and knowledge. Worked with the same guy in ME5506, we actually met in this mod first, then we decided to work together on ME5506 too.

Actually end up coming away learning tricks product designers have used to reduce the total number of screws in their products. That is very useful for me, I am getting sick of screwing like 20+ bolts on my line following robot.

Learnt quite a bit on analyzing environmental impacts of different components. Main takeaway, or at least according to our group project is: metals use up lots of energy to make-->try use less metal.

Assessment is to get an appliance, tear it down, then analyze it part by part and by weight. Some parts like motors with different metal compositions we just wing it and roughly estimate the composition with ChatGPT's help.

^The hair dryer we dismantled, you think I'm joking? Got it off Carousell at 3 bux, pretty nice deal.

Basically after we analyze the materials and parts with EI99 (no time to figure out how to use the EU approved app), we also need to suggest improvements and I used my EE knowledge for this (ironic, EE saving my ass in what is by all account an ME mod in an ME coursework)

So yeah, I just said lets swap the brushed motor with brushless motor, and raise the "smartness" of the whole thing by introducing microcontrollers into the mix.

This is the result after I redesigned the casing. Our suggestions:

  1. Changing heating element to something more efficient
  2. Changing default brushed motor to brushless motor (BLDC)
  3. More adjustable settings
  4. Improving repairability
  5. Detachable power cord
  6. Capacitive buttons

Got an A for this, which is very nice of them.
