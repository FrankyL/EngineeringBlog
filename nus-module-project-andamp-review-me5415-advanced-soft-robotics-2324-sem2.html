[NUS Module Project &amp; Review] ME5415 Advanced Soft Robotics (2324-SEM2)

Franky Laurentis March 24, 2025

Soft robotics is a pretty good name for the module and what to expect. One of the better ME M.Sc. modules I have taken

More of a conceptual module to share more about soft robotics, how it works and how it is being applied industrially/in real life (irl)

1st part is origami robotics, applications and the maths that went into it.

Yoshimura tessellation, there's a lot more varieties and tessellation patterns, basically these stuff will be heavily tested for the mid term quiz, it's "open-book" but not even chatgpt will be useful for long

Examples of Miura-ori, Yoshimura and Waterbomb tessellations. Personally, I like the Miura-ori, very versatile and I have applied it for an expandable, electrically controlled lantern, though the result is crap as I used the normal A4 paper for it.

2nd part is silicon-esque robotics, this is the SOFT soft robotics. Complicated maths too, and more simulation heavy if you are into that. There is also elaboration on various methods on how these soft robots can be made to move, from the complicated shape memory alloys (SMA) or straight-up just good-ole internal pulleys

Octopus inspired soft robot

Final assessment has a survey report (ezpz cuz we don't procrastinate for once) and then there's also a simulation thing with 2 questions: 1st is on a kitchen-like environment with multiple type of food with different shapes and 2nd is on a 3D maze with pipes and various obstacles the robot is supposed to negotiate through.

The teams (mine picked the 1st one) are supposed to simulate the soft robot we designed, and we legit racked our brains on where to even begin with like half a semester left. So as a backup, I just 3D printed a gripper with TPU and just grabbed stuff with different sizes as a placeholder for "simulation", just in case we can't get it done. We failed to get the sim done lol.

Turns out, basically no one succeeded in the simulation part except for like 1 other group, and we technically did because real life demo superseded the simulation anyway. The remaining groups, really just used PowerPoint animations to "simulate" what their robot is supposed to do, not sure how well they did in the grading, but I honestly don't think PowerPoint animation should count as "simulation", but that's prof. Laschi's prerogative.

How we looked the whole presentation session

I was like eh, how do we counter a Sausages, Orange Juice Box, Meatballs, Broccoli, Carrots, Green Beans, Spaghetti Noodles, Cookies and Fried eggs (sunny side up) shaped objects? I just categorized them into meatball type, sausage type, beans type, and heavy juice box and then we work from there:

At first, it's just 3 layers, but it sucked at the beans type and not enough grip for the heavier items, so I made it into 5, still not that good for beans, but it's way better for the heavy juice box case.

Later added a small scoop to salvage the beans case, it's quite a tough edge-case if you know what I mean, but whatever works

This one is a variant for servo control, end up not being used but I guess I tried.
