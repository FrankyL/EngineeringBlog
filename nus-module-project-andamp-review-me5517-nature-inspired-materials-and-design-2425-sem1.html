[NUS Module Project &amp; Review] ME5517 Nature-Inspired Materials and Design (2425-SEM1)

Franky Laurentis March 25, 2025

Module name described most of what it's all about. Topics are: Structural Design Elements, Layered Structures, Adhesives, Structural Colors, Self-healing materials, Super-wettability, Biological Elastomers and Circular Economy.

There is a guest lecturer too in my semester, quite a chill guy. If I remember correctly, the lectures are also done through Zoom so that students missing the lecture can still interact with the lecture.

25% grade is on a closed book quiz with 2 page cheat sheet, not that easy, the module is actually very dense in terms of information. In terms of the long term takeaway, the module is less about the dense info and more about examples of how researchers can get inspired from nature how to apply the knowledge.

75% is on a group project about the application of nature inspired idea (15% interim presentation, 30% final presentation, 30% materials/report). While it sounded easy, it ended up being really time consuming, I advise you to work on it as soon as the profs/lecturers approve of your group's topic.

Need to also consider your topic carefully. My group's topic is originally on the application of coconut skin due to it's anti corrosive property and resistance against the sea environment. However after some reading, we found out the anti-corrosive property is due to the structure of multi-layered cellulose. This means our project need to somehow adopt the cellulose structure to duplicate the anti-corrosive property, similar to how a research group developed a synthetic version of mussel glue by replicating the molecular structure. The scope is too tough for us students so we end up needing to come up with something else.

End up deciding on doing something about using seashell shapes against dust storm on Mars or something, assuming the grooves are biologically evolved to withstand coastal waves. The prof end up quite liking our idea, turns out what he mainly wanted is just something that is novel for once.

The interim presentation got quite intense though when he started grilling a group for reviewing another paper instead of coming up with something new. Deadass threatened to give them the zero if they don't change what they are doing.

My sketch of a Martian Seashell inspired habitat, I envisioned the dome to be able to rotate to face towards the dust storm.

End up being unused though lol

Honestly the module quite good if you are interested in material science and the many ways you can get inspiration from nature to solve your problems.
