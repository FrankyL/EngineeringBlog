On getting NUS M.Sc Mechanical Engineering

Franky Laurentis June 5, 2025 Is NUS Mechanical Engineering Masters worth it?

Grades overall: 4.3/5, way higher than I initially expected

2324SEM1:

ME5401 Linear Systems: B-

ME5405 Machine Vision B

2324SEM2:

ME5404 Neural Networks B+

ME5415 Advanced Soft Robotics A

2425SEM1:

ME5517 Nature-inspired Materials and Design A-

ME5608 Additive and Non-Conventional Manufacturing Processes A-

ME6505 Engineering Materials in Medicine A-

2425SEM2:

ME5406 Deep Learning for Robotics A+

ME5506 Corrosion of Materials B+

ME5611 Sustainable Product Design & Manufacturing A

Got whacked pretty hard in 1st sem of this masters coursework, thought I still had the groove to just tank double technical heavy mods straight away after like 4 years out of school.

Honestly in hind sight, linear systems should have the disclaimer on it that it's supposed to be toughest control systems mod in all unis. My MA1505 and MA1506 back in Bachelor's did not touch linear algebra at all, and that is absolutely a nightmare when I hop straight into linear systems.

So I have to rate based on sheer difficulty:

Really very hard, do not do this to yourself:

ME5401 Linear Systems

Same difficulty as the usual technical engineering mods, i.e. it's quite alright if you are comfy performing at "A-" level of effort in Engineering:

ME5405 Machine Vision

ME5404 Neural Networks

ME5406 Deep Learning for Robotics (to be fair, project 2 is a shitshow because of the structure of how it was assessed and my teammates)

Easy because I have extra skillsets, might be tough if I am pure ME background, "Depends on your skills/wildcard" difficulty:

ME5415 Advanced Soft Robotics

ME5611 Sustainable Product Design & Manufacturing

As long as you don't procrastinate, cuz the work is time-consuming, i.e. "don't be a dumbass" difficulty:

ME5517 Nature-inspired Materials and Design

ME5608 Additive and Non-Conventional Manufacturing Processes

ME6505 Engineering Materials in Medicine

"Use some common sense and should be able to clear quite easily" easy:

ME5506 Corrosion of Materials

Some of the robotics mod is actually not available for ME coursework and that should have been noted more clearly would be my main complaint, but on the other hand, I used their slot to take material science / additive manufacturing mods which has proven very useful in my daily job

Soft robotics + Nature design + Additive Manufacturing + Deep learning sounds like a very potent combo to me. Might build some kneecap support prosthetic soon when I am more free or bored again.

ME6505 Engineering materials in medicine is alright, learned lots about stroke, and with ageing parents, I think the knowledge might come in handy.

Overall, I am surprised with the so called "meme" mods, but I actually quite like these project-based assessment. Typically in exam-based assessments, one prep for exam, blasted their load in 1 go and forget everything in like 2 weeks. Project work actually asked for problem solving with the skillset I currently have and it allows for more flexibility and creativity. 10/10 pretty fun, would highly recommend.

These mods are "easier" in some respect because I have no issue with presentation and report writing. Maybe students who sucked at English and lacking in presentation experience will abhors project assessments, but for now, I prefer it.

Also, after taking several "meme" sounding mods, it actually changed my opinion on these type of modules. Allowing students to breathe a bit and digest the information they have been presented with is helpful, in my opinion. I remember back then during my B.Eng. the rush to complete tutorials and homeworks after every lectures, then the grind on past year papers and then rushing to complete the cheat sheet for the exam, just to forget most of them like 1 month later feels like a gigantic waste of time.

This might be slightly offensive too, but ngl I think the cutting edge info mods like soft robotics/additive manufacturing/nature designs will serve me way better than "let's learn how to replicate Q-learning from scratch on Python within 2 weeks".

Not saying that technical mods are not good, but I think they can be re-contextualized for a more project-based implementation, you can teach the formulas and how it works, but maybe relax the requirements on the project so that they can just whack python libs instead, y'know what I'm saying. Focus more on the robot that is going to execute the trained Q-learning network instead of the Q-learning itself kinda? Like focus more on wtf u trying to use it for? Like I didn't know why tf is BJT significant until I graduated, bro just say it's used as a switch.

OK rant over, another point to note too that Soft Robotics and Sustainability might be easier to me cuz I have product design, PCB design / EE and 3D printing exp already. It is easy for me because of external skillset. Don't go around taking these mods thinking it's easy and then getting screwed cuz you are pure ME with ME work exp, it 100% will not end well for you.

I think I will stop here for now, was planning to discuss about people who asked me "Why ME M.Sc? Why not CS, why not Masters By Research, why not PhD?", but it will make this article wayyyy too long.
