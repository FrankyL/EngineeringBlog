Ribbed LED Lamp

Franky Laurentis September 9, 2025

Got this idea from some dude on Instagram

Used PETG for most of the build, very similar to the Concert LED Glow Stick in terms of circuitry but reliant on USB for power instead of battery

Still using the ESP32C3 SuperMini

Try 1: Ribbed design

Pretty complicated design (overengineered it a bit)

There's these 4 rib supports that is vertical

In the pic above is the ribs attached to the rib supports, might need some hot glue gun as the peg turns out to be quite short and the rib might pop upwards with little force

Above shows the light cover that will encircle the LED base. Honestly, I done goofed a bit during the build cuz I'm overly excited, but the procedure should be:

    1. Light Cover + rib supports
    2. 19cm ribs
    3. 17cm ribs
    4. 15cm ribs
    5. 13cm ribs
    6. 11cm ribs
    7. Lastly, then we add the LED base

Total items:

    • 3x 19cm diameter rib
    • 2x 17cm diameter rib
    • 2x 15cm diameter rib
    • 2x 13cm diameter rib
    • 2x 11cm diameter rib
    • 4x vertical rib supports
    • 1x light cover
    • 1x LED base

Try 2: Simplified Curving Cuboid Light Cover + LED Base

Here is the 1st alternative light cover, turns out it's a good idea to make the LED core/base to be modular.

In this case, I basically recycled the LED base, but simplified the overall design, no more ribs, just reuse the Light Cover from Try 1 and make it curve in Blender.

It looks way nicer than initially expected, looks pretty "artsy" if I may say so myself

Try 3: Hexagon Lattice Cylindrical Light Cover + LED Base

This the 3rd and last one I made for now, with fancy hexagon lattice pattern on cylinder.
