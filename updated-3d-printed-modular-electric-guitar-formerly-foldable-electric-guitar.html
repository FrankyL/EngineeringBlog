[UPDATED] 3D Printed Modular Electric Guitar (Formerly: Foldable Electric Guitar)

Franky Laurentis June 20, 2025

LEMME SHOW YOU WHAT THE ABYSS OF INSANITY LOOKS LIKE

Should I just buy a 2nd hand Squier or Ibanez or Pacifica to begin with? Yes.

Is making a whole guitar myself incredibly troublesome? Also Yes.

Why am I doing this? I dunno man, the guitar gods is telling me a modular guitar (as in swap out the pickups, not the neck, I am not that insane) sounded like a cool idea so here I am. I will definitely offend guitar traditionalists, man I was seen like a heretic when I said headless guitars make more sense in terms of the physics (and looked cooler) and now here I go making straight up modular guitars.

Man, I can't even play guitars...I can play piano and recorder a bit and even then it's half-assed at best.

I thought I can save a couple of hundred bucks on a guitar that I 3D-printed (cuz I'm a cheapo), it turned into a multi stage saga that spanned years (mostly due to procrastination, also cuz of an actual job) that goes into deadends a few times, where I went back to the drawing board to study actual mechanical principles, then consulting ChatGPT for hours on end trying to understand multiple configurations of 3D prints + carbon fiber tubes. Don't knock it till you try it, the suggestions it spewed is pretty solid and matches up with what I am already aware of.

I will also honestly say this is the toughest project I have done yet. Main reason being this is also the first time electronics took a backseat and mechanical principles is 80% of the work, as someone who is used to winging the mechanical design and casings without considering strength and flex of anything, this is very new but fascinating to me.

I damn nearly give up and about to just buy a damn neck, before I read up on how to "finish" the neck and holy shit it's still troublesome, just in a different way.

*sigh* Had to buy a PETG filament (ChatGPT suggested like Nylon and Carbon filaments too, which sounds expensive), and adjust the temperatures on Cura (PETG is 240/80, PLA is 192/60, ABS 230/100). First time using this filament, pretty cool, stronger than PLA and not as pain in the ass as ABS. ABS is supposedly the most "overpowered" but without a cover to maintain temperature, it's the most troublesome of the trio I mentioned above (also you won't believe the number of glue sticks I bought from Popular just to make sure the damn print won't wrap). The higher hotplate temperature occasionally also caused the microcontroller in the 3D printer to freeze and whoop-dee-doo there goes your progress.

V1. Failure of failures

This is the failed V1, supposed to be a foldable neck. Yes, I didn't read up on jack shit before starting V1, I know. Totally underestimated how much tension is being exerted on the whole neck, it detuned and bent on its own when I tried tuning for the 1st time ever. Horrible idea and blasted my self confidence back to the stone age :'^( Just joking, I had to put this aside for months as I moved house and joined a new job, then just getting distracted by 3-4 other projects and actual job.

Also, if you seen the image, you will notice that it is made to fold away from the string / fretboard side, the strings exerted so much tension it actually bent it in the opposite direction, into the fretboard direction.

V2. Overcompensation

I deadass fed this image to ChatGPT and try to get its opinion on the positioning of CF tubes

There's a truss rod (darker reddish orange thing between the 2 bright orange rods) on the upper 3/4 of the neck (Already, this deviates from the norm where the truss rod is firmly implanted the whole length of the neck, total of 4 CF tubes (2* OD10ID8 and 2* OD6ID4)

I split it into 4 parts for the neck: [BOTTOM] [MIDDLE] [TOP] [HEAD]

Printed BTM and MID at 80%, then TOP and HEAD at 90%. Changed the settings cuz I reckoned TOP and HEAD are thinner by design, has thinner CF tubes and the truss rod will not be epoxy'd will need the extra strength wherever it can get it. (Also TOP didn't take too long to print anyway)

V2 Body

Above is the body with the module inserted, I was going for a C-shaped body (still retain the right half of the telecaster shape, copied the measurements from another madman who made a 3D model LOL, I think he only wants to use it as a prop, not an actual guitar) and the module is inserted from the left. The module is going to contain the whole circuit, from the telecaster pickups, audio jack, the selector switch, and the 3 knobs for volume and tone.

Above is how it looks like with the module removed. You can see the guiding rail on the body now. There's 2 more CF tubes (OD6 ID4) reinforced prints behind the C shaped body to help resist the tension and toughen the whole print. This whole guitar will actually be on the thicker side with the back support.

Top X-ray view here, the long cylinder that extends into the body is the space for the CF tubes. I have split the whole body frame into TOP-MID-LOWRIGHT-LOWLEFT. TOP is connected to the neck, a CF tube will extend down through TOP-MID-LOWRIGHT.

The LOW (LOWRIGHT and LOWLEFT combined) part (rightmost in this rotated image) is initially way too huge for my liking and it will take forever to print, so I split them, but since the CF tube only went into LOWRIGHT, I have to connect LOWRIGHT and LOWLEFT in a sturdy way, thus 6 screws and nuts should do the job, I will also use epoxy + higher infill at the connection.

The Back support is shown here in this side view where it will act as an extra support frame for the C shape, the support connects TOP and LOWLEFT in this case. I was hoping that it will be a direct stiffening support to resist against tension when there is no module in place.
