[UPDATED] ESP32 Based BLE Modular Arcade Controller (Akecons アケコン)

Franky Laurentis June 18, 2025 3D Printed Modular Gamepad

From here on out, I will just refer to these arcade controllers as akecons (アケコン, akekon) cuz typing the long name every time is really tedious. Why do I make this? Just nostalgia haha.

Main Module contains ESP32, this is also the "action buttons" side of the akecon. Connection and magnets is on the left side, shape is a bit weird cuz I am optimizing for space so that it's not as big as the typical akecons. Also I copied the measurements wholesale from a diagram I found on Google. There are lots of variants for the button arrangements, and I picked the one that looked the nicest and ergonomic.

The magnets is one of those door magnets you can get online. I used them during my research engineer stint at SIT and dayum they are strong. Action buttons are bought online too, they are pretty cheap.

Honestly, pretty straightforward in terms of circuitry, literally just solder the 8 action buttons to a PCB with ESP32. The ESP32 will just keep scanning for changes of state, yada yada. The main obstacle that actually got me to waste lots of time is the BT identifier thingie. The one that tells the PC what is it connected to? IIRC they are called something like "HID (Human Interface Device) Descriptor"

At first it was just some random numbers and it confused the PC, but after I mimicked the HID descriptor of my Logitech gamepad, it worked perfectly.

Changeable Module without ESP32, basically the "secondary" module, it is the "dumb" side and the connection is on the right, and I used the steel plates that comes with the door magnets I bought online.

The joystick part is also purchased online, and it's the ones used in commercial arcade machines. I honestly thought it's going to be analog like the PS2 joysticks, but it turns out to be digital, 4 contact switches for each direction. So I end up just treating the joystick side as if they are buttons too.

This is a variant of the Changeable module, this one uses 4 buttons and also a total of 5 pins on the connection to do the same thing as the joystick module. Yes, this is basically the "gamepad arrow buttons" on typical gaming controllers.

After I gave it more thought, I came to the realization that perhaps it would have been better to use 7-8 pins instead of 5 for the inter-module connection. 5 pins cuz GND + 4 directions (short sighted af, I know, I also want to whack the past me.)

If it got 8 pins, it means I can have total 7 pins on the left "dumb" module. Perhaps I2C (OLED screens and stuff) + analog stick is possible in that case.

YT vid here is showing connection test between akecon modules, action buttons and joystick test

Doing a quick gameplay on a lightweight game, the response time is decent, not some top tier wired gaming keyboard level thing, responsive enough that I can have a decent time in PVP.

Also if you are wondering why is there a USB wire when I clearly mentioned BT HID Descriptor? Just for 5V power from the USB. Back then I didn't put in too much thought on the battery side, just want a quick prototype and planned for a power module at the back of the main akecon module, but haven't got around to making one.
